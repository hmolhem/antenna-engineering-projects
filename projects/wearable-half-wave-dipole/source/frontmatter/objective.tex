\chapter*{Objective}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Objective}

The objective of this project is to design, simulate, and evaluate a wearable half-wave dipole antenna at \(1.120~\mathrm{GHz}\). A center-fed cylindrical PEC dipole is first established and tuned in free space. The same antenna is then evaluated above a simplified finite multilayer body model composed of skin, fat, and muscle.

The study quantifies how antenna-body separations of \(10~\mathrm{mm}\), \(5~\mathrm{mm}\), and \(0.1~\mathrm{mm}\) affect the reflection coefficient \(S_{11}\), the dominant matching-minimum frequency, radiation efficiency, total efficiency, gain, and two-dimensional and three-dimensional radiation patterns. A first-order redesign is then performed for the \(5~\mathrm{mm}\) case by shortening the dipole and evaluating a feed-gap variation.

The scope is electromagnetic antenna performance. The finite tissue model is used as a lossy loading environment; biological safety and SAR compliance are outside the scope of this report.

\chapter*{Portfolio Context}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Portfolio Context}

This report documents a complete engineering workflow for a wearable half-wave dipole: analytical initialization, free-space tuning, construction of a simplified lossy multilayer body model, controlled antenna-body spacing studies, radiation-performance comparison, and first-order retuning near the body.

The evidence in this report is \textbf{analytical and simulated}. No fabricated prototype, VNA measurement, chamber measurement, or SAR validation is claimed. CST-generated results are reported as simulation outputs for the documented finite geometry, solver settings, material model, and computational boundaries.

The 0.1~mm separation case produced a non-monotonic displacement of the dominant matching minimum. A formal mesh- and boundary-convergence study was not completed because of CST Learning Edition limits. That frequency displacement is therefore classified as a fixed-model observation rather than a convergence-validated trend. The severe target-frequency mismatch, efficiency loss, and gain reduction are retained as the principal engineering observations from the extreme-clearance case.

Native CST project binaries are not included in the public package. Selected figures, model dimensions, simulation notes, analytical scripts, and the complete report source are provided to support technical review and reproducibility within the stated limitations.
